Securing Africa’s Food Future Through Strategic Agricultural Solutions

Agriculture constitutes the backbone of most African economies. An overwhelming majority of the continents poor rely on the sector for their livelihood. African agriculture is commonly characterized as being in crisis, but there are also successes. The greatest failure is that food production has not kept pace with population growth. KPSSL provides the strategic and managerial solutions to address these issues.

From Food Exporter to Importer: Reclaiming Africa's Agricultural Potential

African agriculture has struggled persistently, with former food-exporting nations now dependent on imports. This decline reflects a perfect storm: runaway population growth outpacing productivity gains, compounded by dietary changes, environmental pressures, and - most critically - addressable management deficiencies that demand urgent intervention.
